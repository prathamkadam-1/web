Financial Structure

How we are funded

Pratham Kadam Trust does not solicit or accept public donations. Our independence is our integrity.

Sole funding source
Entirely from the personal earnings of the founder. The trust does not solicit or accept any external or public contributions.
Long-term corpus goal
To build a self-sustaining financial corpus over time, ensuring support for children across generations without outside reliance.
Governance commitment
Mission continuity and child welfare above all personal interests. Trustees are bound to uphold this at every decision.
Operating philosophy
Pilot-first. Deep, documented, measurable impact on a small cohort before any expansion. Quality and transparency are non-negotiable.
First-year plan

Building step by step

A deliberate, grounded approach — every milestone earned before the next is attempted.

01
Register the Trust
Legal trust registration under the Indian Trusts Act, PAN obtainment, and formal establishment of the charitable entity.
Completed
02
Banking & Governance
Open a dedicated trust bank account and establish formal governance procedures and trustee responsibilities.
Completed
03
Beneficiary Selection
Define clear, fair criteria for identifying beneficiaries. Identify the first small cohort of orphaned children aged 5–18.
04
Launch Pilot Program
Begin comprehensive support for the first cohort across all six pillars — education, health, nutrition, digital access, mentorship, and career guidance.
05
Document & Measure
Maintain detailed records of every child's progress. Publish transparent outcome reports. Evaluate before any expansion.
